In this edition’s summary: Dark clouds are gathering over the Sahara, and record rainfall is hitting that part of the world. In Niger, the mosque of Zinder has not withstood the impact. Still on the African continent, 200,000 doses of vaccine against the Mpox disease — formerly known as monkeypox — will arrive as early as tomorrow in the Democratic Republic of Congo. In this news bulletin as well, the British Prime Minister’s apology to the victims of a deadly fire. Seven years ago, in a London tower, 72 people died in the blaze. And finally, in France: the country is still waiting to learn who will make up its new government. As early as this evening, the Secretary General of the Élysée could announce the names of the ministers. That’s the headline summary. Welcome to the news.
