Futur proche

Describes immediate or near future actions. Very common in everyday speech. Equivalent to English “going to do something”.

Planned action something you intend to do

  • Je vais appeler ma sœur. I'm going to call my sister.
  • Il va commencer demain. He's going to start tomorrow.
  • On va faire les courses. We're going grocery shopping.
  • Je vais voir ce qu'il me reste. I'll see what I have left.
  • Nous allons changer ça. We are going to change that.

Immediate future about to happen very soon

  • Attention, ça va tomber ! Watch out, it's going to fall!
  • Je vais vomir... I'm going to throw up...
  • Le train va partir. The train is about to leave.

Prediction based on current evidence

  • Il va pleuvoir. It's going to rain.
  • Elle va dire non, je le sens. She's going to say no, I can feel it.
  • Ça va être compliqué. That's going to be complicated.

Uncertainty / Possibility predicting or wondering about what might happen

  • Je sais pas si ça va marcher. I don't know if it's going to work.
  • Tu crois que ça va suffire ? Do you think it's going to be enough?
  • Je me demande si ça va aller. I wonder if it’s going to be okay.
  • On verra comment ça va se passer. We’ll see how it’s going to go.

informal scheduling When talking about upcoming events

  • On va manger chez eux samedi. We’re going to eat at their place Saturday.
  • Je vais travailler demain. I'm going to work tomorrow.
  • Tu vas venir avec nous ? Are you going to come with us?

Formation

Structure

subject + aller (present) + infinitive

  • Je vais partir. I'm going to leave.
  • Tu vas comprendre. You're going to understand.
  • Il va pleuvoir. It's going to rain.
  • Nous allons manger. We’re going to eat.
  • Vous allez voir. You're going to see.
  • Ils vont venir. They're going to come.

Notes

  • This is the **spoken future** in French — more natural than futur simple in everyday talk.
  • Works with **reflexive verbs**: Je vais me lever à 7h.
  • Negative: Place ne and pas around aller
    → Je ne vais pas sortir.

Signal words

ce soir, demain, bientôt, tout à l’heure, dans une minute, la semaine prochaine, ce week-end, dans un instant, d’ici peu …

Negation — ne … pas / jamais / plus

  • Je ne vais pas venir. I'm not going to come.
  • Il ne va jamais changer. He's never going to change.
  • On ne va plus sortir avec eux. We're no longer going to go out with them.

Questions — intonation / est-ce que / inversion

  • Tu vas venir ? Are you going to come?
  • Est-ce que vous allez sortir ? Are you going to go out?
  • Va-t-il pleuvoir ? Is it going to rain?

Présent vs. Futur proche

  • Présent: action in progress — Je mange. I'm eating.
  • Futur proche: imminent or scheduled — Je vais manger. I'm going to eat.
