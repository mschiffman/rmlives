PRO-TIPS & COMMON MISTAKES

Master these key differences between French and English vowels:

  • Don't glide:
    • English vowels often glide (day = /de…™/). French vowels stay pure throughout.
  • Lip rounding matters:
    • French uses lip position more distinctively than English. Round lips for /y/, /u/, /√∏/, /≈ì/, /o/.
  • Tension and precision:
    • French vowels are more tense and precise than English. Keep muscles engaged.
  • The /y/ sound:
    • Most difficult for English speakers. Practice: "ee" with rounded lips. Say "tu" NOT "too"!
  • Y is both a vowel and a consonant in French:
    • Between two vowels, it is pronounced as the consonant /j/, as in voyager.
  • Closed vs Open:
    • Pay attention to closed (/e/, /o/, /√∏/) vs open (/…õ/, /…î/, /≈ì/) distinctions - they change meaning!
