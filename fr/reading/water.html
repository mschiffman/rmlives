 English

The water cycle is a continuous natural process that allows water to circulate on Earth. Under the effect of the sun, water from oceans and lakes evaporates and forms water vapor. This rises into the atmosphere, cools down, and condenses into clouds.

When the droplets become too heavy, they fall back as precipitation: rain, snow, or hail. The water then seeps into the ground to replenish underground water tables or joins rivers that flow toward the oceans. Part of the water is also released by plants through evapotranspiration. Thus, water constantly returns to its starting point, sustaining life on our planet.
