Common French Exclamations

Click a category to see natural, everyday exclamations (safe, polite, and useful in casual speech).

"Purée !" /pyʀe/

Family-friendly “Jeez!” — super common.
Purée, J'ai raté mon train ! → “Jeez, I missed my train!”

"Punaise !" /pynεz/

Like “Darn!” or “Jeez!”
Punaise, j’ai oublié mes clés ! → "Darn, I I forgot my keys!”

"Mince !"

Gentle frustration.
Mince, encore en retard… → “Jeez, late again…”

"Oh là là !"

Like a Swiss-army knife with many uses: surprise, dismay, amazement—tone does everything.
Oh là là, il y a du monde. → “Wow, there are a lot of people.”



"Ça alors !"

“Well I’ll be!” / “No way!” — pleasant or shocked surprise.
Ça alors ! Tu es déjà là ? → "Well I'll be! You're already here?”

"Bah(/Ben) dis donc !"

Colloquial “Wow, would you look at that!” (friendly, informal).
Ben dis donc ! Tu as changé de coiffure ! /kwafyr]/ → "Wow! You changed your hairstyle!”

"Eh ben !"

“Well then!” → light surprise or a hint of disbelief depending on tone.
Eh ben, tant mieux. → "Well, that’s good then.” / “Well, good for you.” / “Oh, that’s great.”

"Waouh !" /wa.u/

Modern “Wow!” (also spelled Wouah / Waou).
Waouh, c'est joli, j'adore ! → "Wow, that's pretty, I love it!”



"Non mais !"

“Oh, come on!” / “Seriously?!” (mild scolding).
Non mais, t’as vu le prix ? → "Come on, did you see the price?”

"Ça suffit !"

“That’s enough!” — clear boundary-setting, still polite.

Ça suffit, arrête de m'appeler. → "That's enough, stop calling me.”

"Zut !"

Old-school “Darn!” — harmless and handy.

Zut ! Le magasin est fermé. → "Darn! The store is closed.”

""Bon sang !" /sɑ̃/

Colorful but not rude: “Good grief!” / “For goodness’ sake!”

Bon sang, j'en étais sûre ! → "Damn, I was sure of it!”



"Trop bien !"

“So good!” / “Awesome!” — very casual and current.
Trop bien, tu l’as trouvé. → "Awesome! You found it!”

"Génial !"

“Great!” / “Brilliant!”
Génial ! J'ai eu le poste ! /pɔst/ → "Awesome! I got the job!”

"Super !"

Exceptional!” / “Great!” — very natural in daily speech.
Super ! Tu viens avec nous ! → "Great! You are coming with us.”

"Extra !"

Great!” / “Terrific!” — common for pleasant surprise, delight, or enthusiasm.
Extra ! C'est exactement ce que je voulais. → "Terrific! That's exactly what I wanted.”

"Youpi !"

Yippee!” / Hooray!” — exclamation of joy.
Youpi ! C'est les vacances ! /vakɑ̃s/→ "Yay! It's vacation time.”



"Dieu merci !" /djø mɛʀsi/

“Thank God!” — classic expression of relief or gratitude.

Dieu merci, tout s’est bien passé ! → “Thank God, everything went well!”

"Merci mon Dieu !"

“Thank you, my God!” — stronger and more emotional than
Merci mon Dieu ! Tout le monde va bien. → “Thank God! Everyone's okay.”.

"Ouf !" /uf/

“Phew!” — for relief after tension or a narrow escape.
Ouf, j’ai cru qu’on allait le rater ! → “Phew, I thought we were going to miss it.”

"Enfin !"

“Finally!” — relief that something is over or solved.
Enfin, c’est terminé ! → “Finally, it’s done!”

"On l'a échappé belle !" /eʃape/

Literally “We escaped it beautifully” → “That was a close one!” Used when you avoided trouble or danger.
On l'a échappé belle ! Un arbre est tombé juste derrière notre voiture. → “That was close! A tree fell right behind our car.”



"Mon Dieu !" /mɔ̃ djø/

“My God!” — for shock, disbelief, or distress.
Mon Dieu, qu’est-ce qui s’est passé ? → “My God, what happened?”

"Oh mon Dieu !"

“Oh my God!” — identical tone to English “OMG,” very common in speech.
"Oh mon Dieu, qu'est-ce que j'ai fait ?" → “Oh my God, what have I done?”

"C’est pas vrai !"

“No way!” / “It can’t be true!” — very common and natural for shock or disbelief.
C’est pas vrai ! Il a gagné ! /ɡaɲe/ → “No way! He won!”

"Quelle horreur !" /ɔʀœʀ/

“How awful!” / “What a horror!” — used for something tragic, disgusting, or appalling.
"Quelle horreur, le pauvre !" /povʀ/ → “How awful, poor guy!”

"Incroyable !"

“Unbelievable!” — can express amazement or shock depending on tone.
"Incroyable ! Personne ne l'a vu venir." → “Unbelievable! Nobody saw it coming.”



"Sapristi !" /sapristi/

Playfully old-timey “Goodness me!”

"Fichtre !" /fiʃtr/

Stage-French astonishment; fun in writing or joking around. "My gosh!"
