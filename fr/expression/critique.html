Expressions of critique & observation in French

Click the buttons below to find the most common ways to express critique & observation in French:

"Tu radotes."

You’re repeating yourself.

"On dirait un disque rayé."

You sound like a broken record.

"Toujours la même rengaine !"

Same old tune!



"Je suis pas convaincu(e)."

I’m not convinced.

"Mouais…"

Ehh… (non-committal skepticism).

"C’est pas terrible."

It’s not great.

"Bof."

Meh.



"T’exagères."

You’re overdoing it.

"Calme-toi, on respire."

Take it easy, let’s breathe.

"Fais pas tout un drame."

Don’t make a big deal out of it.



"Arrête de râler."

Stop complaining.

"Tu chipotes pour rien."

You’re nitpicking for nothing.

"Toujours à critiquer…"

Always criticizing…



"Sérieux ?"

Seriously?

"Tu rêves."

You’re dreaming / no way.

"On va pas se mentir…"

Let’s be honest… (soft preface to a blunt point).



"Arrête de te la raconter."

Stop showing off / bragging.

"Il/Elle se la pète."

He/She’s full of themself. (colloquial)



"Tu te contredis, là."

You’re contradicting yourself.

"Décide-toi."

Make up your mind.



"Franchement ?"

Honestly? (prelude to a candid remark)

"C’est pas faux."

Not wrong. (dry agreement/observation)

"Pas bête."

Not a bad point.
