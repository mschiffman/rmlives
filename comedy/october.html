Filipino time

Have you ever heard of "Filipino Time"? I've learned it's a wonderfully elastic concept, and I have two perfect examples to prove it.

Example A: The Deadline

In graduate school, I once found my Filipino friends frantically typing away. "What are you guys doing?" I asked. "Our assignment!" they replied. "It’s due at noon." I checked my watch. "But... it is already noon." They looked at me like I was crazy. "Exactly. That’s why we’re starting now."

Example B: The ETA

A friend of mine perfectly translated the concept for me. He said when his Filipino wife texts him, "I'm on the way," it actually means, "I'm just about to hop in the shower." So, if you're ever waiting on someone running on Filipino Time, maybe order an appetizer while you wait.

My smooth dad

My dad's first words upon waking up from a three-day coma might be the worst — and also the best — pick-up line of all time.

Back when he was a handsome young captain training to be a pilot in England, he had a mishap: he crash-landed his plane during a solo flight. He was laid up in the officer's ward, in a coma.

Meanwhile, a rumor was circulating among the nurses about the handsome Burmese pilot. One nurse from another hospital was so intrigued she begged a friend to sneak her in for a peek. As fate would have it, just as this beautiful, blond, blue-eyed nurse in all white was leaning over him, my dad’s eyes fluttered open.

His first words back in the land of the living, you would never guess.

He said, "Oh, I died, huh?"

Smooth, dad. Real smooth.

Where Is My Husband?

I was a very naive recent university grad, working for the commercial attaché at an embassy. One afternoon, his wife called. "Where is my husband?" she asked. Without thinking, I blurted out, "What do you mean? He walked home for lunch an hour ago." (His house was literally across the street.)

He didn’t return that day. A coworker informed me that "lunch" was code for "visiting his mistress." Whoops.

The next morning, my boss walked in with scratches on his face. He immediately called me into his office and shut the door. This is it, I thought, I'm being fired. He pointed to his face. "Do you see these scratches?" he asked. "Yes, sir," I whispered. He looked me dead in the eye and said, "I want you to know... I fell in the shower last night. That's all. Go back to work."

It was the most gloriously terrible lie I had ever heard.

Helpful neighbor

I once tried to be a helpful neighbor and ended up giving advice to a burglar. My heart was in the right place, even if my brain was on vacation.

I was in my apartment when I heard someone jiggling the doorknob next door. When I opened my door to help, I saw a man with his back to me. In my most helpful voice, I announced, "Excuse me! There's no one at home right now. You'll have to come back this evening."

Instead of thanking me, the man nearly jumped out of his skin and then sprinted down the stairs. I was genuinely confused. I mean, common courtesy would be an, "Oh, thank you!" right?

It took me a solid minute to connect the dots: Oh, he was trying to break in. He was VERY, VERY AWARE that no one was home. Whoops.

Impressing the In-Laws

Learning a new language is a minefield, where one tiny mistake in tone can change absolutely everything. Case in point:

A close relative of Robert, eager to impress his fiancée's Chinese relatives, decided to show off his language skills at a family gathering. He stood up, puffed out his chest, and proudly announced to the entire room, "I am a Gwai Lo (龜佬)!"

A collective gasp, followed by stunned silence. Jaws were on the floor.

You see, he meant to say Gweilo (鬼佬), a common term for a white man. A noble effort. However, what he actually said was Gwai Lo (龜佬), which translates to... well, an impotent man.

So, in his grand attempt to bond with his new family, he essentially declared his inability to continue the family lineage.

I'm sure the in-laws were very surprised when the couple produced a son!

Sleepy driver

My husband always takes the wheel on long trips. This has conditioned me to believe my only job on the highway is to be an expert-level napper.

So, as I was driving my best friend from LA to Vegas, sans Robert, I casually said, "Hey, do me a favor and wake me up when I fall asleep."

The look of sheer, unadulterated terror on her face was a sight to behold. "What do you MEAN?!" she shrieked. "How can you fall asleep while you're DRIVING?! How would I even know when you’re asleep?"

Trying to be helpful, I replied, "Oh, that's easy. The car will start to shake and play havoc. My brain is just wired for napping on long trips."

She spent the next four hours giving me a non-stop, rapid-fire briefing on every major news story, celebrity gossip, and political development she could think of.

We arrived in Las Vegas safely, and I arrived more informed than I had been in years about news from Myanmar. Who needs the news when you have a terrified best friend for a co-pilot?

Indigestible love

Growing up, I was definitely Dad's favorite. Like, THE favorite. But then I started cooking, badly. 😢

One evening, I set the table with a flourish, announcing that dinner was ready. My dad looked at the plate, and said, “Oh, my little darling daughter cooked today. I'm… actually not that hungry. How about I just have coffee and cookies?”

I tried to protest—“But Dad, I made it with love!” He just patted my hand gently and replied, “Sweetheart, love is not always digestible.”

Even the favorite child can't escape the truth of being a terrible cook!
